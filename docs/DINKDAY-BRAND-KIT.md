# DinkDay Brand Kit

Date: 2026-06-04
Status: Phase 1 brand foundation
Project codename: PicklePal
Public brand: DinkDay

## Brand Decision Summary

DinkDay is the public V2 name for the app currently known internally as PicklePal.

The brand promise:

```text
Game day, handled.
```

Supporting line:

```text
From first serve to final recap.
```

DinkDay helps casual pickleball groups run the whole game day: players, rotations, scoring, leaderboards, match history, awards, and recaps.

## Phase 1a: Name

Chosen name:

```text
DinkDay
```

Why it works:

- It is short, memorable, and easy to say.
- It is pickleball-native without sounding too technical.
- It frames the product around a whole game day, not only scoring.
- It fits friend groups and casual clubs better than a serious tournament-style name.
- It gives the brand room to support scoring, rotations, recaps, and social moments.

Naming caveats:

- Final domain, social handle, and trademark checks are still required before public launch.
- "Dink" gives the name personality, but the rest of the brand should avoid becoming childish or joke-heavy.
- PicklePal remains the internal project codename until code, docs, metadata, and launch assets are renamed.

## Phase 1b: Brand System

### Personality

Primary personality:

```text
Friendly Game-Day Organizer
```

DinkDay should feel like the person in the group who calmly gets everyone on court, keeps the score honest, rotates players fairly, and still makes the recap fun afterward.

Competitive energy is secondary. Rankings, MVP, hottest duo, and recaps should add excitement without making the app feel intimidating.

### Visual Direction

Visual direction:

```text
Fresh Court Day
```

DinkDay should look bright, clean, and made for daylight play.

Use:

- Crisp court-line structure.
- Court green and sky blue accents.
- White or near-white daylight surfaces.
- Large readable scores.
- Clean spacing and quick-scanning layouts.
- Selective celebration color for awards and wins.

Avoid:

- Dark sports-betting energy.
- Heavy admin dashboards.
- Dense gray card stacks.
- Mascot-heavy or cartoonish pickleball jokes.
- Overly intense tournament broadcast styling.

### Logo Direction

Logo direction:

```text
Wordmark first
```

The primary logo should be a clean DinkDay wordmark. A small court-line, paddle, ball, or rotation detail can support the wordmark, but the name should carry the brand.

The supporting mark may later become the app icon, favicon, or social avatar.

### Colors

Color direction:

```text
Court Green + Sky Blue
```

Recommended palette roles:

- Primary: court green.
- Secondary: sky blue.
- Base: white and near-white daylight surfaces.
- Text: dark charcoal or deep green-black.
- Celebration accent: warm yellow for wins, MVP, streaks, and recap moments.

Color rules:

- Keep the app bright and outdoor-readable.
- Do not make the whole product one green gradient.
- Use warm accents sparingly so celebration moments feel earned.
- Keep contrast strong for phone and tablet use outdoors.

### Typography

Typography direction:

```text
Rounded athletic sans
```

The main type should feel friendly, readable, modern, and slightly sporty.

Scores, ranks, win rates, and recap stats can use a stronger numeric style, but the product should not look like a betting board or tournament broadcast.

### Voice

Core voice:

```text
Clear + friendly
```

Core UI should be direct:

- Start Game Day
- Add Player
- Save Match
- End Session
- Create Your Group
- View Demo Group

Celebration moments can be more expressive:

- Crown today's MVP
- Hottest duo
- Best match
- Ready for your next DinkDay?

Voice rules:

- Be practical first.
- Add personality around recap, awards, and social moments.
- Avoid startup buzzwords.
- Avoid forced pickleball puns.
- Use humor lightly, like real group-game chaos.

### Iconography

DinkDay should use icons to make the UI quick to scan and more polished.

Recommended icon concepts:

- Trophy for MVP and wins.
- Medal or badge for rankings.
- Users for players and groups.
- Paddle, ball, or court mark for game-day context.
- Clock for history and recent matches.
- Sparkles for recap moments.
- Share and download icons for social assets.

Icon rules:

- Prefer familiar symbols over text-heavy controls.
- Use icons to clarify actions, not decorate every surface.
- Keep icon style consistent across navigation, buttons, cards, and recaps.

### Visual Assets

Asset direction:

```text
Mixed system
```

Use real, bright pickleball court photography for public-facing marketing and social proof. Use clean court graphics, player chips, score layouts, and UI-native visuals inside the product.

Recaps can blend both: branded overlays that look good on top of real game-day photos.

## Phase 1c: Landing Page Plan

### Landing Page Goal

The public landing page at `/` should explain DinkDay quickly and make the product feel real.

Primary message:

```text
Keep the games moving from first serve to final recap.
```

### Hero

Hero structure:

- Brand name: DinkDay.
- Headline/tagline: Game day, handled.
- Supporting line: Keep the games moving from first serve to final recap.
- Primary CTA: Create Your Group.
- Secondary CTA: View Demo Group.
- First viewport visual: live scoring preview.

The hero should show a bright real pickleball court or game-day image paired with a clear phone or tablet preview of the live scoring screen.

Avoid abstract illustrations or generic dashboard previews in the first viewport.

### Page Structure

Recommended page flow:

1. Hero with live scoring preview.
2. Funny group-chaos problem section.
3. Workflow story.
4. Compact feature grid.
5. Demo group preview.
6. Public beta proof section.
7. Final CTA.

### Problem Tone

Tone:

```text
Funny group chaos
```

Example lines:

- Stop asking "who's serving?" every three points.
- No more mystery scores.
- No more accidental rematches.
- No more group-chat archaeology after the games.
- DinkDay keeps the day moving while everyone else just plays.

Use this humor lightly. The page should feel relatable, not gimmicky.

### Workflow Story

The first explanatory section should tell the game-day story:

1. Pick who's playing.
2. Rotate fairly.
3. Score live.
4. Recap the day.

This should come before a generic feature grid so the page feels built around a real pickleball flow.

### Feature Grid

Use clear feature headings with playful supporting copy.

Recommended features:

- Live Scoring: Tap who won the rally. DinkDay handles the score, server, side-outs, and win condition.
- Fair Rotations: Keep teams fresh and sit-outs balanced without doing math between games.
- Leaderboards: Track wins, losses, win rate, and point differential across every game day.
- Match History: Save completed games so nobody has to reconstruct scores later.
- Recaps: Crown MVP, hottest duo, and best match before the group chat asks.

### Demo Group

The demo group should be fully fake.

Demo rules:

- Do not expose real friend-group data.
- Use fictional players and sessions.
- Use attractive avatar colors and realistic sample scores.
- Include enough history to show the product at its best.
- Show active game day, leaderboard, recent matches, MVP, and recap content.

### Proof Strategy

Use:

```text
Demo product proof + public beta framing
```

Because DinkDay will not yet have public testimonials, credibility should come from clear demo product visuals and realistic sample data.

Suggested copy:

```text
Built for real pickleball crews. Now opening public beta.
```

### Final CTA

Final CTA:

```text
Ready for your next DinkDay?
```

Supporting copy:

```text
Create your group, invite your crew, and keep the games moving from first serve to final recap.
```

Buttons:

- Create Your Group
- View Demo Group

## Brand Guardrails

DinkDay must avoid:

- Looking like a corporate SaaS admin portal.
- Turning "dink" into a childish joke.
- Feeling like an elite tournament app.
- Using dark gambling or sports-betting visual cues.
- Hiding the actual product behind vague marketing language.
- Overusing cards, tables, borders, and gray panels.

DinkDay should feel:

- Friendly.
- Useful.
- Courtside-ready.
- Bright.
- Social.
- Organized.
- Lightly competitive.

## Open Checks Before Public Launch

Before the public brand is final, complete:

- Domain availability verification.
- Social handle search.
- USPTO or relevant trademark search.
- Competitor search for pickleball, padel, tennis, and recreational sports apps.
- Logo exploration.
- App icon exploration.
- Demo group content creation.
- Landing page image and UI preview selection.
